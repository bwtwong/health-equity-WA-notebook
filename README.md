## The introduction

Health equity has the evidence and the people. It is starting to have the finance.

What it needs now is better architecture: a clearer view of how the pieces connect, where the gaps are, and how good work becomes sustainable impact at scale.

This hub maps how money moves, what has been tried, where the leverage points are, and who is already working on it. It treats finance, systems, and collective memory as health equity tools.(Simply put: powered-search of stories from the Internet)

Positionality: I'm not a First Nations person. I came to this through research and stayed because I believe community empowerment works best when it is structurally supported with the right capital, the right institutions, decisions made by people closest to where impact lands.

This is a navigator, not an endorsement. It is a living map for anyone who wants to connect what exists, scale what works, and contribute their piece.

*Come in from wherever you are.*

## About this map

This hub was built by a researcher and an AI (Claude, by Anthropic) as a navigation tool — not a verified database. Content has been compiled from publicly available sources on the internet, with direct links provided wherever possible so you can go deeper and check for yourself.

It has not been independently fact-checked. Organisations, programs, and figures reflect what was findable and current at time of build — but things change, funders shift priorities, and programs end.

**Use this as a launchpad, not a landing place.** Follow the links. Talk to the organisations directly. Verify before you act.

Inclusion of any organisation is not an endorsement. It reflects active work in health equity, education, or community empowerment at time of research — nothing more.

## Inclusion criteria

Every organisation, mechanism, and pathway in this hub meets at least one of the following:

1. **Health equity** — active programs or investment targeting First Nations or broader health equity outcomes
2. **Education** — investment in knowledge, capability, or workforce development at community level
3. **Community empowerment** — anything that increases community agency, structural capacity, or financial independence: legal empowerment, Indigenous-controlled capital, cooperative models, data sovereignty, political voice, economic participation

Inclusion is not an endorsement of broader organisational practices, investment performance, or values. Where relevant, individual nodes carry specific caveats.

## Framework

### The five columns

```
RESEARCH → SOLUTIONS → PATHWAYS → FINANCE → OUTCOMES
```

**Research & evidence** — the documented knowledge base. What we know, who produced it, where the gaps are.

**Solutions** — split into two arms:
- *Technical* — the intervention, program, tool, or clinical model
- *Adaptive* — the relational and cultural work that makes technical solutions land

**Pathways** — how solutions reach communities: ACCHOs, system reform, policy, workforce

**Finance** — split across the same technical/adaptive lens:
- *Adaptive finance* — patient, trust-based, long-term philanthropy and Indigenous-controlled capital
- *Technical finance* — outcomes-based, measurable: SIBs, blended finance vehicles

**Outcomes** — health equity, self-determination, SROI, global replication

### The adaptive/technical distinction

Technical solutions deployed without adaptive work consistently fail. Adaptive work without technical solutions does not scale. They require different evidence standards, different measurement frameworks, and different finance mechanisms. Naming this distinction is itself a derisking mechanism for investors.

### Barriers & facilitators

Sit across every column — systemic racism, data sovereignty gaps, short funding cycles, geographic isolation, the illegibility of adaptive work to investors — and their counterparts.

## The story layer (Layer 1)

**A — The gap** — the problem, stated plainly. Research exists. Health equity does not. Something breaks in between.

**C — The system** — possibility. The ecosystem already exists. Researchers, organisations, finance mechanisms, and communities are all working on this — rarely in full view of each other.

**B — A pathway** — one thread animated from evidence to outcome. Change has a shape. The journey is navigable.

**D — The invitation** — the user enters with a lens: researcher, investor, community organisation, or policymaker.

## Platform architecture

```
/platform              ← shared framework code
  /layer1              ← story entry point
  /layer2              ← navigation hub
  /components          ← shared UI components

/instances
  /wa-health-equity    ← this instance
  /[future-instance]   ← ASEAN market access, global Indigenous health, climate-health...
```

Content lives in data files separate from code. Adding a new instance is a content swap, not a rebuild.

## Principles

- **Open source, not platform-locked** — the code is public, the content is editable, no vendor controls it
- **AI-assisted, not AI-governed** — AI helps people navigate, it does not make decisions about communities or priorities
- **Positionality first** — who built this, why, and from what position is named at the entry point
- **Living document** — this will change, and that is the point
- **Routes toward, not around** — every pathway directs people toward First Nations-led organisations and community-controlled structures
