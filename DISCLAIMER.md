# Disclaimer & Inclusion Criteria

## What this hub/notebook is

A navigation tool --- NOT a verified database, NOT an endorsement directory, NOT a comprehensive map.

It was built by a curious researcher and an AI (Claude, by Anthropic) using publicly available sources on the internet. It has not been independently fact-checked. Direct links are provided wherever possible so you can verify information at the source.

**Use this as a launchpad, not a landing place.**

---

## What "included" means

Every organisation, mechanism, or pathway in this hub was included because it met at least one of the following criteria at time of research:

| Criteria | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Health equity** | Active programs or investment targeting First Nations or broader health equity outcomes |
| **Education** | Investment in knowledge, capability, or workforce development at community level |
| **Community empowerment** | Anything that increases community agency, structural capacity, or financial independence — including legal empowerment, Indigenous-controlled capital, cooperative models, data sovereignty, political voice, and economic participation |

---

## What "included" does not mean

- It is not a recommendation to work with, invest in, or partner with any listed organisation
- It is not a verification of any organisation's claims, impact figures, or financial performance
- It is not an assessment of any organisation's cultural safety practices, governance, or values alignment
- It is not a complete or exhaustive list of organisations working in this space

---

## Node-level caveats

Some nodes carry specific caveats where relevant. For example, large philanthropic funders with complex or contested histories, or organisations whose inclusion reflects one specific program rather than their broader work. These caveats are visible on the node itself.

---

## Data provenance

Content was compiled from:
- Organisation websites (linked directly in nodes)
- Publicly available reports and publications
- Government and peak body data
- Academic and grey literature

Sources were current at time of build (June 2026). Things change --- funders shift priorities, programs end, new organisations emerge.

---

## How to correct or contribute

This is an open map. If you believe:
- An organisation has been incorrectly described
- An organisation should be added or removed
- A figure or fact is out of date
- A caveat is missing or wrong

Please open an issue or pull request on GitHub. Corrections from people with direct knowledge of organisations or communities are especially welcome.

---

## A note on AI

This hub uses the Claude API (Anthropic) to provide contextual navigation, answering questions about nodes, suggesting connections, going deeper on specific topics.

AI responses are generated in real time and are not fact-checked. They reflect Claude's training data and are subject to the same limitations as any AI system,  including potential inaccuracies, gaps, and biases. Please use AI responses as a starting point for your own research, not as authoritative information.
