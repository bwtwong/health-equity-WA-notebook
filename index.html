Research → Impact Hub · First Nations Health Equity · Western Australia

Health equity has the evidence.
It has the people.
It's starting to have the finance.

What it needs now is better architecture — a clearer view of how the pieces connect, where the gaps are, and how good work becomes sustainable impact at scale.

This hub maps how money moves, what has been tried, where the leverage points are, and who is already working on it. It treats finance, systems, and collective memory as health equity tools.

I'm not a First Nations person. I came to this through research and stay because I believe community empowerment works best when it is structurally supported — the right capital, the right institutions, decisions made closest to where impact lands.

A navigator, not an endorsement. A living map for anyone who wants to connect what exists, scale what works, and contribute their piece.

A — The gap

The knowledge exists.
The health equity doesn't.

First Nations communities in Western Australia have been researched, documented, and reported on for decades. The evidence base is rich. The health gap persists. Something is breaking between what we know and what changes.

8.6
Year life expectancy gap, First Nations vs non-Indigenous Australians
5×

C — The system

The ecosystem already exists.
It just isn't connected yet.

Across WA and Australia there are researchers, community organisations, health services, policy frameworks, finance mechanisms, and social ventures — all working toward the same thing, rarely in full view of each other. This hub makes the whole system visible.

B — A pathway

Change has a shape.
Here's one way it moves.

Follow one thread from evidence to impact — ear health in remote WA. The journey is navigable when technical and adaptive work together, and when finance structures match the timescale of real change.

Evidence: middle ear disease burden
KAHRA research · Kimberley & Pilbara · preventable hearing loss in children
↓
Technical solution: mobile ear health clinics
Earbus Foundation WA · specialist equipment · outreach across 6 WA regions
↓
Adaptive solution: community co-design
Schools + health + culture working together · family engagement · trust-building over time
↓
Pathway: ACCHO delivery + school networks
KAMS · Mawarnkarra · community health workers reaching children where they are
↓
Finance: blended — mining sector + philanthropy + govt
Fortescue · Roy Hill · Telethon7 · FRRR · long-term program funding

Outcome: hearing restored · education outcomes improve
